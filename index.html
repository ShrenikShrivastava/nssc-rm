Keynote Speakers

Dr. N Sudheer Kumar

Ex-Chairman, ISRO

“Cryogenic Engines and Its Future”

Day 0 · Oct 27 | 18:30 – 20:00 Kalidas Auditorium

Dr. Sudheer Kumar, former Chairman of ISRO's Launch Vehicle Programme, unveils the science behind India's most powerful cryogenic engines. He will discuss how these engines enable deep-space missions like Chandrayaan and Gaganyaan, and what the next generation of propulsion holds for interplanetary travel.

Padma Shri awardee, led the development of C25 cryogenic stage for GSLV MkIII.

Mr. Shubhanshu

Crew Captain, Gaganyaan

“Extraterrestrial Mining”

Day 1 · Oct 28 | 18:30 – 20:00 Netaji Auditorium

As a key astronaut candidate for India's first crewed space mission, Mr. Shubhanshu brings a unique perspective on space resource utilization. He will explore the technologies required to mine asteroids and the Moon – from robotic excavators to in‑situ fuel production – and how this will fuel a sustainable space economy.

Wing Commander, IAF; selected for Gaganyaan mission; specialist in extravehicular activity.

Prof. Impey & Dr. Emma

NASA & Academia

“Advancements & The Space Craze”

Day 2 · Oct 29 | 15:00 – 16:30 Kalidas Auditorium

Prof. Impey (University of Arizona) and Dr. Emma (NASA JPL) join forces to discuss the explosion of public interest in space – from SpaceX starships to Artemis lunar missions. They analyze how social media, private enterprise, and new discoveries are fueling a second Space Age, and what it means for future scientists and engineers.

Prof. Impey: award-winning astronomy educator. Dr. Emma: lead systems engineer for Mars Sample Return.
